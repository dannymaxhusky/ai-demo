Definition of Fair Wear and Tear

Fair wear and tear refers to natural and unavoidable cosmetic wear typically arising during normal operation and business use, as determined by the supplier.

  • Equipment remains in good working order without damage arising from unnatural use in a normal business environment.
  • All components, screens and monitors remain usable and functional for expected work.
  • Fair wear and tear generally covers minor scratches, slight keyboard wear and normal battery degradation over time.

Exclusions from Fair Wear and Tear

  • Misuse, accident, unauthorized modification, improper installation, damage or mishandling.
  • Willful or negligent damage.
  • Operation in unsuitable environments or contrary to manufacturer documentation.
  • Natural disaster exposure, power surge, discharges, or unauthorized maintenance.
  • Cracked or broken screens.
  • Missing keys or damaged keyboard.
  • Water damage.
  • Physical casing damage, including dents, cracks and significant scratches.
  • Battery swelling or improper-use battery damage.
  • Internal damage not due to regular wear.

Cosmetic Grade Criteria

GradeDefect Description
AVery good condition: no chips, scratches, screen/key defects or worn palm rests/touchpads.
BGood condition with normal wear and tear: micro scratches on chassis/screen that are invisible from 8 inches away or when powered on.
CScratch and dent: wear and tear on chassis, keys, touchpad, palm rest and screen.
DBroken or missing plastics, major structural damage, broken or missing hinges.
